Science Lesson (Medium)

  • Photosynthesis: Plants absorb carbon dioxide from the air and use it to make food through photosynthesis.
  • Atom Structure: The nucleus is the dense center of an atom, containing protons and neutrons.
  • Eye Function: The iris controls how much light enters the eye by adjusting the size of the pupil.
  • White Blood Cells: White blood cells defend the body against infection and disease.
  • Insulin Production: The pancreas produces insulin, which helps control blood sugar levels.
  • Atmosphere Composition: Nitrogen makes up the largest portion of Earth's atmosphere—about 78%.
  • Friction: Friction is a force that resists motion between two surfaces in contact.
  • Force Unit: The Newton (N) is the SI unit used to measure force.
  • Plant Roots: Roots absorb water and nutrients from the soil to help the plant grow.
  • Gases: Gases have no fixed shape or volume—they expand to fill their container.
